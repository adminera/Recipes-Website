Description

Steak, a quintessential favorite among meat lovers, epitomizes culinary indulgence with its rich, savory flavor and tender texture. Sourced from the prime cuts of beef, such as ribeye, filet mignon, or sirloin, steak offers a variety of taste experiences depending on the cut and preparation method. Whether grilled to perfection with a smoky char, seared in a sizzling hot pan for a caramelized crust, or broiled for a balanced cook, each technique brings out the unique qualities of the meat, making steak a versatile and cherished dish.

The appeal of steak extends beyond its taste, tapping into a sense of tradition and celebration. Often the centerpiece of festive meals and special occasions, a well-cooked steak embodies the art of fine dining and the joy of savoring a meticulously prepared meal. This blend of robust flavor, texture, and the ability to elevate any dining experience makes steak an enduring symbol of culinary excellence.

Ingredients

  • Beef Steak
  • Salt
  • Black Pepper
  • Olive Oil or Butter
  • Garlic Cloves

Steps to cook

  1. Season Steak: Generously season both sides of the steak with salt and black pepper
  2. Heat Pan: Preheat a heavy skillet or grill over high heat until very hot.
  3. Cook Steak: Add olive oil or butter to the pan, then place the steak in the pan, cooking for 3-4 minutes on each side for medium-rare, or to your desired doneness.
  4. Add Aromatics: In the last minute of cooking, add garlic cloves to the pan for extra flavor.
  5. Rest Steak: Remove the steak from the pan and let it rest for 5 minutes before slicing and serving.
